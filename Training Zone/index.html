They're not pretty, but with your help, they can be manageable.

Here are some thoughts on how to deal with it:

  1. • Realise that it's up to you. You are responsible for making it work.
  2. • Attend one lecture and get the notes from the other (start with the web notes, but that's not enough).
  3. • Find a friend in the one you miss and get them to tell you what else was covered in class that's not on the web.
  4. Which one do you miss? - Some ideas to help you with that decision are:
    • o If you miss one out of two lectures for a subject, that's 50%. If you miss one out of 3 that's 33%.
    • o Which one are you most on top of?
    • o Which subject has the most complete lecture notes?
  5. • Don't ask your lecturer - "did I miss anything in the lecture?" If you didn't miss anything, there'd be no point to lectures
